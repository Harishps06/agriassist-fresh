Vegetable Farming in Kerala - Complete Guide

COMMON VEGETABLES IN KERALA:
- Tomato (തക്കാളി)
- Brinjal (വഴുതന)
- Okra (വെണ്ട)
- Bitter Gourd (പാവയ്ക്ക)
- Snake Gourd (പടവലം)
- Cucumber (വെള്ളരി)
- Green Chilli (പച്ചമുളക്)
- Onion (ഉള്ളി)
- Carrot (കാരറ്റ്)
- Cabbage (മുട്ടക്കോസ്)

PLANTING SEASONS:
- Kharif Season (June-September): Most vegetables
- Rabi Season (October-March): Winter vegetables
- Summer Season (March-May): Heat-tolerant varieties

SOIL PREPARATION:
- Deep plowing 2-3 times
- Add organic manure (FYM) 25-30 tons/hectare
- Maintain pH 6.0-7.0
- Good drainage essential

WATERING REQUIREMENTS:
- Young plants: Light watering daily
- Mature plants: 2-3 times per week
- Avoid waterlogging
- Use drip irrigation for water conservation

COMMON PESTS AND DISEASES:
- Aphids: Use neem oil spray
- Whitefly: Yellow sticky traps
- Fruit borer: Hand picking + neem spray
- Powdery mildew: Sulfur dusting
- Bacterial wilt: Crop rotation

FERTILIZER SCHEDULE:
- Basal: NPK 20:20:20 @ 2kg/100sqm
- Top dressing: Urea @ 1kg/100sqm every 15 days
- Organic: Compost, vermicompost, poultry manure

HARVESTING:
- Harvest in early morning
- Handle carefully to avoid damage
- Store in cool, dry place
- Most vegetables ready in 60-90 days

ORGANIC FARMING TIPS:
- Use neem cake for pest control
- Intercropping with marigold
- Crop rotation every season
- Compost tea for plant nutrition
